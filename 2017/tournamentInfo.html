MN State Tournament Information - 2017

Once your group has registered as a member of Odyssey of the Mind, it's time to start preparing for the Minnesota State Tournament!

Our tournament, and our whole organization, is completely run by volunteers. We require each team to provide a volunteer or judge to help our tournament run smoothly -- please keep this in mind as you look ahead to the state tournament. Find more information about this requirement here.

The 2017 MN Odyssey of the Mind State Tournament will be held on:

March 11, 2017 at
Heritage Middle School
121 Butler Ave W
West St Paul, MN 55118.

  • Tournament Schedule
    • Please note your teams' Long-term performance times, Spontaneous times, and weigh-in times (problem 4 only).
    • Our schedule is very compressed. Please be sure that your team is on-time (i.e. EARLY) to all of their scheduled competitions.
    • If you believe there is a mistake with the scheduling of your team, please contact Hannah Nelson immediately.
  • Tournament Information - This document has all of the critical information about the tournament including scheduling, parking, food, sales, and more! A few notable things to point out:
    • Teams should be in their staging area with all props and paperwork 20 minutes before their scheduled long term performance time.
    • Please remind your teams' judges and volunteers that they are to report for their assigned duties on-time.
    • Judging and Volunteer assignments will be distributed in the coming weeks.
    • NEW THIS YEAR- Each performance site will have a reserved seat for a photographer/videographer in the front row of the audience seating. Each team may choose to use this seat to ensure that photos and/or videos are captured for the team.
  • School Map and Parking Map See the attached map to see your problems' performance site. (color) (black and white)
    • Please note that many of our performance sites are upstairs. There is one elevator in the building. This elevator should be used ONLY for moving large props and for anyone that is unable to use the stairs. Please keep the elevator available for those who truly need it.
    • As a reminder, parents are able to help move props within the school and even into the staging area. After the performance, parents can also help move props out of the performance area.
    • There are designated areas for parking and prop drop-off.
    • Please distribute this map to parents and anyone attending the competition!
